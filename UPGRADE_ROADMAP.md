# Journal-upgrade roadmap

This document turns the referee recommendation into an implementable research
programme. The current estimand remains a static tax--benefit response to an
imposed labour-income shock. The upgrades below add a defensible trade-to-
labour first stage without silently presenting assumptions as estimates.

## Target estimand

For worker or household (i), estimate the change in annual earnings,
employment, hours, inactivity, and disposable income caused by a change in
US destination-sector demand generated by the tariff schedule. The fiscal
incidence calculation then maps the predicted labour-market outcomes through
PolicyEngine UK.

## Work packages

### A. Sector--destination exposure

Build (E_{jdt}=Export_{jdt}/Turnover_{jt}) from HMRC trade and ONS business
data, retaining destination (not only total US exposure). Construct tariff-
induced demand changes with destination-specific elasticities and document
alternative elasticities as scenario parameters.

Required inputs: historical HMRC exports by SIC and destination, tariff
changes, ONS turnover/employment, and concordance versions.

### B. Labour-market first stage

Estimate event-study or shift-share specifications using pre-shock exposure:

```
outcome_it = worker_i + time_t + sector_time + beta * exposure_it + controls + error_it
```

Outcomes: earnings, employment, hours, unemployment, inactivity,
self-employment, and re-employment. Cluster inference at the sector-region
or sector-destination level and report leave-one-out exposure robustness.

Required inputs: longitudinal worker or employer--worker data. The current
cross-sectional FRS cannot identify this coefficient by itself.

### C. Heterogeneous worker allocation

Use first-stage predictions to replace independent SIC-only Bernoulli draws.
At minimum stratify by age, sex, education, occupation, region, earnings
decile, and household structure. Calibrate predicted aggregate losses to the
sector totals so the model preserves the current accounting identity.

### D. Household price channel

Add household expenditure shares and a tariff-price mapping. Report nominal
income, real disposable income, poverty, inequality, and indirect-tax effects
separately. Do not combine the channel with the wage channel unless the
incidence assumptions are explicitly stated.

### E. Partial-equilibrium production bridge

Before a full CGE model, add imported-input exposure, export-demand response,
sector output/value-added changes, and labour-demand elasticities. Keep the
existing wage-cut/displacement families as bounds around this bridge.

### F. Validation and uncertainty

Increase headline assignment draws, report numerical Monte Carlo standard
errors separately from assignment dispersion, and use bootstrap or replicate
weights when the licensed survey design permits it. Validate sector aggregates
against historical trade-shock episodes.

## What cannot be completed from the current repository alone

The causal first stage, worker mobility estimates, and survey-design
uncertainty require licensed longitudinal or linked employer--employee data.
The current FRS snapshot can support the tax--benefit mapping and calibrated
heterogeneity scenarios, but it cannot identify dynamic labour-market effects.
Those estimates must be added as external inputs or a separate empirical
module rather than simulated as if observed.

## Submission positioning after upgrades

With A--C, the paper becomes an applied trade/public-finance study. With D--E,
it becomes a broader household-welfare exercise. A full general-equilibrium
contribution would require a separate quantitative trade model and should not
be implied by the current PolicyEngine simulation alone.
