\begin{abstract}
\noindent Who bears a foreign tariff once the tax--benefit system responds? I derive sector-level earnings shocks from the 2025 tariff schedule and US-export intensity by SIC division, and pass them through PolicyEngine UK on Family Resources Survey microdata. Holding the aggregate loss fixed, I vary the adjustment margin across four families. The system cushions the poles similarly---39 per cent of a displacement shock against 41 per cent of an earnings-equivalent wage cut---but through opposite instruments. Wage-cut protection is automatic: income tax delivers 35 of those 41 points, and the rate is exactly invariant to benefit take-up. Job-loss protection is contingent: Universal Credit carries a quarter of it, and the rate swings seven points across plausible take-up rates while the tax component never moves. Insurance against job loss is comparably generous but far less reliable. Nationally poverty rises 0.047 points; among affected households, from 4 to 38 per cent.
\end{abstract}
