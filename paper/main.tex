\begin{abstract}
\noindent This paper asks how the UK tax--benefit system responds to alternative labour-income stress scenarios calibrated to the sectoral exposure of the 2025 US tariffs. It imposes wage, displacement, inactivity and reallocation scenarios on Family Resources Survey households and evaluates the resulting statutory fiscal-incidence changes in PolicyEngine UK. The exercise is static, partial-equilibrium and first-round. It does not model the transmission from tariffs through export prices and quantities, production, value added, productivity and labour demand; instead, it imposes a reduced-form tariff-to-wage-bill bridge. Neither that bridge nor the allocation between wages, employment and inactivity is causally estimated. Results are conditional tax--benefit stress tests, not estimates of the tariffs' macroeconomic, productivity, employment or total household-welfare effects. Assignment dispersion is reported separately from statistical uncertainty.
\end{abstract}
