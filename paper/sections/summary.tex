\section*{Executive summary}

This paper estimates a conditional fiscal-incidence response, not the causal
economic effect of a tariff. Under the full schedule, the imposed direct
earnings stress is \pounds0.886 billion per year. When the expected aggregate
loss is realised as a wage cut, the tax--benefit system offsets
\FullWageCushion\ per cent and the Exchequer cost is
\pounds\FullWageExchequer\ billion. Under Bernoulli displacement, the
\ProductionDraws-assignment mean offsets \FullDisplacedCushion\ per cent,
costs \pounds\FullDisplacedExchequer\ billion, and raises relative BHC poverty
by \FullDisplacedPoverty\ percentage points. Earnings equivalence holds in
expectation, not assignment by assignment. These figures are conditional on
the imposed stress and adjustment margin; they are not tariff forecasts.

The policy implication is narrow: automatic tax relief is
available immediately after an earnings cut, whereas protection after job loss
depends on benefit-unit eligibility and take-up. Temporary wage insurance,
faster benefit enrolment, and targeted work allowances are candidate
counterfactuals, not findings of the present simulations. Their fiscal costs
and distributional effects require dedicated runs and explicit financing
assumptions.
